# Product Truth: Regenerative AI Water Broadcast

Date: 2026-06-02

## One Sentence

Regenerative AI Water Broadcast is a public message: use AI deliberately, reduce waste, measure honestly, and direct some of the leverage into visible water-cycle restoration.

## User And Job

- User: AI builders, local-first builders, water-cycle/restoration people, and potential collaborators who feel AI environmental guilt but still see practical value in AI.
- Job: offer a concrete, non-greenwash response: efficient use plus visible repair, not abstract offset claims.

## Truth Levels

### Proven

- `docs/regenerative-ai-water-broadcast.md` defines the core thesis and credibility guardrails.
- The message explicitly rejects claims that AI is harmless, that water work cancels emissions, or that generic offsets solve the problem.
- The first audio-ad target in `docs/audio-advert-generator.md` is the water message.
- Checkdam-related docs already provide local language around water retention, check dams, infiltration, flood/drought balance, groundwater recharge, and visible local abundance.

### Working But Unproven

- The thesis is coherent, but the first public post/audio spot is not yet shipped.
- The money pledge language exists, but the legal and governance route is not complete.
- A public ledger is planned, not built.
- Restoration claims still need practitioner review before strong public claims.

### Aspirational

- Fund or start one visible water-cycle restoration action.
- Publish project-level evidence, including location/context, design, maintenance, photos, and failures.
- Invite AI builders to copy the pattern.
- Build a credible public campaign around "Use AI. Repair Water."

### False Or Stale

- "Highest-priority project as of 2026-05-22" is historical, not necessarily current priority.
- This is not proof that AI use is environmentally harmless.
- This is not an offset, carbon credit, or claim that water features cancel compute emissions.

### Private Only

- Some strategic planning, legal decisions, and funding details are private until approved.
- Public copy must avoid overclaiming impact, partners, funding, or legal structure.

## Smallest Honest Pitch

AI has real environmental costs. If we use it, we should use it efficiently and put some of the resulting leverage into visible water-cycle repair: check dams, wetlands, infiltration, rain gardens, and practitioner-reviewed local projects. This is not an offset claim. It is a practical regenerative commitment.
