# Product Truth: Listen

Date: 2026-06-02

## One Sentence

Listen is the Phone Control media snap that helps Peter play local music or spoken word while teaching the system what fits his current state.

## User And Job

- User: Peter listening while working, travelling, or capturing thoughts.
- Job: get usable playback quickly, avoid music that jars the nervous system, and resume spoken-word learning without friction.

## Truth Levels

### Proven

- `docs/listen.md` defines Listen as a snap inside Phone Control Surface, not a separate app.
- Android tests cover Listen guide decisions, queue ordering, teaching events, MediaStore classification, artist queues, album queues, and spoken-word resume.
- The app has Music and Spoken paths, plus pause/skip/artist/album controls.
- Spoken-word position persistence is implemented and tested.
- Listen is part of the installed day-phone app build from commit `76d29ea`.

### Working But Unproven

- Real phone library quality depends on local MediaStore metadata.
- The UI is usable enough to dogfood, but not yet proven as a low-attention daily media player.
- DJ guidance is partly implemented as local teaching/decision logic, not as a mature recommendation engine.

### Aspirational

- Continuum-aware listening that adapts to calm, focus, lift, and open states.
- Richer local teaching signals from user feedback.
- Capture-safe playback, media-session ownership, and headset-button harmony.
- Future ambient signal detection without saving continuous ambient audio by default.

### False Or Stale

- Listen is not yet a public Spotify replacement.
- It is not yet doing real audio analysis or cloud recommendation.
- Older SignalSource prototype language can make the product sound more complete than the current playback surface.

### Private Only

- Peter's music library, listening preferences, Spanish course positions, and feedback signals are private.
- Public copy should use synthetic tracks and scenarios.

## Smallest Honest Pitch

Listen is a private Android media snap inside Phone Control. It plays local music and spoken-word files, remembers spoken-word position, and starts teaching the system which tracks fit Peter's current state. It is an early dogfood media tool, not a finished recommendation product.

## Docs/Copy To Change

1. `docs/listen.md` - separate current playback reality from older SignalSource prototype language.
2. `docs/phone-control-surface.md` - say Listen is a current active snap.
3. Future guides - explain Music versus Spoken behaviour plainly.

## Next Product Move

Dogfood the Music/Spoken split and record the first real friction: metadata, controls, queue shape, or spoken resume.
